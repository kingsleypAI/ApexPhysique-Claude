Principles
Rule 01
Consistency above all
Show up. Missing sessions kills progress faster than bad form.
Rule 02
Progressive overload
Add weight or reps every week. Stagnation means the programme isn't working.
Rule 03
Rest is productive
Thursday off. 8+ hours sleep. Muscle is built during recovery, not during lifting.
Rule 04
Eat to build
High protein. Slight caloric surplus on training days. Fuel the machine.

⚠ Recomposition Strategy · 100kg / 5'10"
At your stats a traditional bulk is unnecessary — you have enough stored energy to fuel muscle growth while in a small deficit. This is called body recomposition. Expect to lose ~0.5–1kg per week while gaining strength. The scale might barely move for weeks — that's muscle replacing fat. Judge progress by the mirror and your lifts, not the number.

Power Oats
Pre-workout breakfast · 22g protein · 96g carbs
5 MIN
▼
Ingredients
120gRolled oats
300mlSemi-skimmed milk
1 largeBanana, sliced
1 tbspHoney (optional)

Steps
1

Perfect Scrambled Eggs on Toast
Post-workout breakfast · 62g protein · high satiety
8 MIN
▼
Ingredients
5 wholeEggs
2 slicesSourdough bread
½Avocado
1 tspButter or olive oil
pinchSalt and black pepper
Steps
1
Put bread in the toaster now — it'll be ready by the time eggs are done.
2
Crack all 5 eggs into a bowl. Add a pinch of salt and pepper. Beat with a fork for 30 seconds until yolks and whites are fully mixed.
3
Put a non-stick pan on medium-low heat (not high — this is the most common beginner mistake). Add butter or oil.
4
Pour in eggs. Stir slowly and constantly with a spatula, moving the egg from the edges to the centre. Takes about 3–4 minutes.
5
Take off the heat just before they look fully set — residual heat finishes the job. They should look soft and slightly glossy.
6
Serve on toast. Slice avocado and place on top. Season with more black pepper.
The rule: Low heat + constant stirring = creamy eggs. High heat = rubbery eggs. Don't rush it.

Lunch & Meal Prep
🍗
Oven Baked Chicken & Rice
Meal prep staple · 57g protein per serving · makes 6 portions
35 MIN
▼
Ingredients (6 portions)
1.8kgChicken breasts
900g dryBasmati rice
2 tbspOlive oil
2 tspGarlic powder
2 tspPaprika (smoked or sweet)
1 tspSalt + black pepper
optionalLemon juice (squeeze over after)
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 200°C. While it heats, line a large baking tray with foil (makes washing up easy).
2
Place chicken on the tray. Drizzle with olive oil, then sprinkle garlic powder, paprika, salt, and pepper over both sides. Use your hands to rub it in.
3
Bake for 22–25 minutes. You'll know it's done when you cut the thickest part and there's no pink inside. When in doubt, give it 3 more minutes.
4
While chicken cooks: boil rice. Pour rice into a pot. Cover with water (roughly double the volume of rice). Bring to boil, then reduce heat to low, lid on, cook 12 minutes. Turn off heat, leave lid on 5 more minutes.
5
Let chicken rest 5 minutes, then slice into strips. Divide chicken and rice across 6 meal prep containers. Add steamed veg to each.
6
Store in fridge up to 5 days, or freeze portions 3–6. Reheat in microwave 2–3 minutes.
Seasoning shortcuts: If you don't want to measure spices, just use any pre-mixed "chicken seasoning" from any supermarket. Results in 10 seconds.

Garlic Beef Mince Rice Bowl
High protein dinner · 55g protein · incredibly easy
15 MIN
▼
Ingredients (1 portion)
250gLean beef mince (5% fat)
150g dryRice (pre-cooked from prep is fine)
3 clovesGarlic (or 1 tsp garlic powder)
1 tbspSoy sauce
1 tspOlive oil
pinchChilli flakes (optional)
200gBroccoli or spinach
Steps
1
Heat oil in a frying pan on medium-high heat. Add minced garlic (or garlic powder) and stir for 30 seconds — you'll smell it instantly.
2
Add mince. Break it apart with a spatula as it cooks — keep it moving so it doesn't clump. Cook for 6–7 minutes until all the pink is gone.
3
Add soy sauce and chilli flakes. Stir for 1 more minute. It'll go dark and glossy — that's the flavour.
4
Microwave broccoli for 3 minutes in a bowl with 2 tbsp of water, covered with a plate.
5
Serve mince over rice with broccoli on the side. Done.
Beginner tip: Soy sauce is your best friend as a new cook — it adds salt, depth, and umami to almost any meat. 1 tbsp goes a long way.

Dinner
🐟
Pan-Seared Salmon & Roast Sweet Potato
Recovery dinner · 55g protein · omega-3 rich
30 MIN
▼
Ingredients (1 portion)
250gSalmon fillet (skin on or off)
200gSweet potato (1 medium)
200gSpinach or kale
1 tbspOlive oil
1 tspGarlic powder
pinchSalt + black pepper
optionalLemon wedge to serve
Steps
1
Preheat oven to 200°C. Cut sweet potato into cubes (about 2cm). No need to peel — just scrub it. Toss in ½ tbsp olive oil, salt and pepper. Spread on a baking tray. Roast 25–30 minutes, turning once halfway.
2
When potato has ~8 minutes left: pat salmon dry with kitchen paper. Season both sides with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
3
Heat remaining oil in a frying pan on medium-high. When oil shimmers (but doesn't smoke), lay salmon in skin-side down if it has skin. Don't touch it for 4 minutes.
4
Flip once and cook 2–3 more minutes. Salmon is done when it flakes easily with a fork and the inside is pale pink (not translucent).
5
While salmon rests: throw spinach into the same hot pan for 1–2 minutes. It wilts fast — just toss it around until it shrinks down.
6
Plate everything together. Squeeze lemon over salmon if you have it.
Don't flip too early: Salmon tells you when it's ready to flip — the flesh will have turned from translucent to opaque about halfway up the side. Flip then, not before.

🥡
High-Protein Chicken Stir Fry
Quick dinner · 60g protein · ready in 15 min
15 MIN
▼
Ingredients (1 portion)
220gChicken breast, sliced thin
150gMixed frozen veg (from bag)
150gPre-cooked rice
1 tbspSoy sauce
1 tspSesame oil (optional but great)
1 tspGarlic powder
1 tspOlive oil
Steps
1
Slice chicken into thin strips — the thinner they are, the faster they cook and the better the texture.
2
Heat olive oil in a pan or wok on high heat. Stir fry = high heat, fast cooking. Add chicken and cook 4–5 minutes, stirring constantly.
3
Add frozen veg straight from the bag. Stir fry for 3–4 minutes until veg is heated through and starting to get slight colour.
4
Add pre-cooked rice, soy sauce, garlic powder, and sesame oil. Toss everything together for 2 minutes until rice is hot and coated.
5
Taste it. Want more flavour? Add more soy sauce, a splash at a time.
Meal prep hack: Make 3x this recipe on Sunday — keeps in the fridge 4 days. Best reheated in a pan for 3 minutes (not microwave, or the rice goes mushy).
